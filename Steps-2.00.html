Choosing a Dataset 2

Things to Consider: Choosing a Dataset for your project is very important.   Here are some things to consider when choosing your dataset.


  • Your project will be shown to the outside world so you want to make sure it's interesting. 
  • You also want to choose something that is interesting to you so you will be passionate about it.
  • You must be able to create at least 2 Industry KPI's from your data set.


Where to go:  You are not limited to where you can get your dataset.  The internet is a great tool for finding datasets.  Colaberry has also generated datasets for you to pick and choose from, but here are some more resources you can use.  Make sure you understand which Industry your data set is tied to because you will need this when picking your Industry KPI's.

Industry KPI's:  While choosing a dataset, you must ensure the data you have ties to an industry KPI.  Remember this is a process so don't rush it.  Finding the right KPI's now will ensure a smoother path to creating your project.

  • https://kpidashboards.com/kpi/
  • https://www.datapine.com/kpi-examples-and-templates/


Combining Datasets:  When using multiple datasets from different locations, ensure you use your basic sql skills when combining datasets.  You can only join on a One to One or One to Many relationship.  A good rule of thumb is for the joining column to be the Primary Key in at least one of the datasets.  You can not join 2 foreign keys together.


Calculations:   Really good KPIs usually come with complex calculations.  Rarely do you get a really good Industry KPI by just summing or averaging a column.   Once you have chosen your KPI's you will need to search the internet for the correct calculations.  Ensure you have all of the column/data to complete the calculation.

Explore : Finding a good dataset can be tough.  Maybe stop and take a different approach.  What's going on in the World today.  Those could serve for interesting projects if you can find enough data for it.  Explore the internet and  be creative.  Remember, you will be sharing these projects on Social Media.  Pick an interesting topic that will interest as many users as possible.  This could lead to a GREAT conversation in an interview.
